

# Customizing The Application Workspace

The Solution Explorer, Layer Manager, Properties Grid, and Block Manager can be closed, re-sized, re-positioned and locked (pinned) based on the user's visual work preferences.

The Build Output, if displayed, can also be closed, re-sized, re-positioned and locked.

Project Pages can be closed, re-sized and maximized.

![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Create%20A%20Solution/UI%20Workspace%20Layout-V2.jpg)

In this example, the Workspace has the Default Layout and default complement of Managers.

## Closing Workspace Managers

1. Click anywhere in the Manager to select it. The Manager's title bar will be displayed in blue when it is selected indicating that this Manager has the active focus:





![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Close%20Manager.jpg)

2. Click on the **X** in the right-hand corner of the Manager's title bar to close the Manager:



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/X%20to%20Close%20Manager.jpg)

3. To re-display the closed Manager, from the menu bar select View and then select the applicable Manager **:**



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Redisplay%20Manager.jpg)

The Manager will be re-displayed in its last used position (and size).

**Caution**: Selecting Restore Default Layout will also re-display the Manager; however,it will cancel any Workspace customization.


## Resizing A Manager's Width

1. Hover the cursor over the Manager's right-hand border.

2. Drag the double-arrowed cursor to right to increase the Manager's width; drag it to the left to decrease the Manager's width:



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Resize%20Manager%20width.jpg)

This action affects the adjacent Manager to the immediate right—and applies to all Managers in the given column.
1. Dragging the double-arrowed cursor to the right decreases the adjacent Manager's width

2. Dragging the double-arrowed cursor to the left increases the adjacent Manager's width.

## Resizing A Manager's Height

1. Hover the cursor over the Manager's top or bottom border.

2. Drag the double-arrowed cursor downward to increase the Manager's height; drag it upward to decrease the Manager's height:



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Resize%20Manager%20height.jpg)



This affects the adjacent Manager immediately below:
1. Dragging the double-arrowed cursor to the downward decreases the adjacent Manager's height

2. Dragging the double-arrowed cursor to the upward increases the adjacent Manager's height.

## Repositioning Managers

1. Click and hold on a Manager's title bar.

2. Drag the Manager to the desired position on the Workspace **.**

3. Position the Manager over the one of the _docking indicator's_ arrows or its center square until a blue highlight shows the Manager's target new position:



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Resposition%20below.jpg)

In this example, the Layer Manager will be re-positioned under the Properties Grid as indicated by the blue highlight.



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/New%20position%20show.jpg)

4. Position the Manager over the docking indicator's center square to re-position the Manager in a tab group with an existing Manager **:**



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Overlaid.jpg)

In this example, the Layer Manager will be re-positioned in a tab group with the Properties Grid **.**





![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Repositioned%20in%20the%20same%20space.jpg)

The Layer Manager is now in a tab group with the Properties Grid and Block Manager.

Click on the applicable tab to display and toggle between the corresponding Managers in the tab group:



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Tabs%20stacked%20managers.jpg)


## Locking & Unlocking Managers

Managers are initially locked (or pinned) to a designated position in the default layout. They can be unlocked and locked.

1. In the Manager's title bar click on the Unlock smart icon to unlock the Manager:



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Unpin%20Manager.jpg)

The unlocked Managers will be displayed as tabs along the left-hand edge of the Workspace:



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Unpinnws%20Tabs.jpg)

In this example, the Layer Manager and Block Manager were unlocked and are displayed as tabs. Click on the applicable tab to display the corresponding Manager.

2. Click on the Lock smart icon to lock the Manager back to its last locked position:



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Repin.jpg)



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Repinned%20to%20orig%20location.jpg)

In the above example, the Layer Manager has been locked back to its lasted locked location. Whereas the Properties Grid remains unlocked and is displayed as a tab.


## Closing Project Pages

1. Click on the **X** in the ProjectPage's tab displayed above the Workspace Canvas:



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Close%20Page.jpg)

2. Double- click on the Page in the Solution Explorer to display it again:



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Display%20Page.jpg)


## Resizing A Project Page's Width

1. Click on the ProjectPage's left-hand border and drag the double-arrowed cursor to the left to increase the page width. This will decrease the adjacent, left-hand side Manager's width.



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Resize%20Page.jpg)

2. Click on the **Project Page**'s right-hand border and drag the double-arrowed cursor to the right to increase the page width. This will decrease the adjacent, right-hand side **Manager**'s width:



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Resize%20Page%20Right%20Hand.jpg)


## Maximizing & Minimizing A Project Page

1. Click on the Maximize smart icon in the Editors pane above the Canvas to maximize the current Project Page **:**



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Maximize%20Page.jpg)

The maximized Project Page will occupy the entire Workspace:





![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Page%20Maximized%20Entire%20Workspace.jpg)

2. Click on the Minimize smart icon in the Editors pane above the Canvas to restore the Workspace and return the current Project Page to its originally displayed size:



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Minimize%20icon.jpg)



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Restored%20Workspace.jpg)


## Restore Default Layout

1. From the menu bar, select View and then Restore Default Layout to restore the Workspace to its default layout and complement of Managers in their default sizes and positions:



![](https://help.crestron.com/construct/Content/Resources/Images/Project%20&%20Pages/Customize%20Workspace/Restore%20Default%20Layout.jpg)
